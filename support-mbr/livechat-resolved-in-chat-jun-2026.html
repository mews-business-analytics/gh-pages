Live-chat “resolved in chat” with no case created

Support only · agent-accepted MIAW sessions · 29-30 June 2026 · built Tue 06/30/2026 | source: bizops.salesforce_fivetran.messaging_session + cs.ada.message · customer names, emails & links auto-redacted

What this is. Salesforce shows ~130 Support live chats “resolved” on 29 Jun. Of those, 64 created a case and 65 resolved in the chat with no case created. This report drills into those caseless chats (65 on 29 Jun + 41 so far on 30 Jun = 106 chats) to test whether each was genuinely resolved — or whether the customer went unresponsive after the agent connected (a missed chat counted as a resolution).

“Resolved in chat” = accepted by an agent, case_id IS NULL, case_creation_requested = false, case_record_type = 'Support'. The key signal below is customer messages AFTER the human agent connected — not raw message counts, which include the pre-handoff chat with the Ada bot.

Key insights

Headline findings
  1. Salesforce reports ~130 Support live chats “resolved” on 29 Jun. That splits into 64 that created a case and 65 resolved in the chat with no case. This report covers the caseless side (106 chats across 29–30 Jun).
  2. ~14.2% of “resolved-in-chat” chats are not real resolutions. 15 of 106 are missed chats — the human agent connected but the customer never replied, and the agent closed for inactivity. They are silently counted as resolutions.
  3. Of those 15 missed chats, 9 got no answer at all (agent only greeted / asked for info / closed) — the strongest case-creation candidates — while 6 did get an answer before the customer went silent.
  4. 71 of 106 are genuine resolutions — the customer engaged after the agent connected (4+ messages) or explicitly thanked the agent.
  5. 13 chats had the agent promise a follow-up / email but no case exists — flagged as needs investigation (did the follow-up happen?), and a case should exist for these regardless.
Data-quality findings
  1. Confirm case existence via the ada_conversation_id → support_cases join, not messaging_session.case_id alone. The session link can be blank even when a case exists, but this is rare and not new (~0.2% of sessions in Jun, ~0% Feb–May — stable since MIAW launch). So almost all “case requested, no case_id” sessions genuinely have no case (resolved in chat, or a true gap), not a linkage error.
  2. case_creation_requested = true does not mean a case was needed — it is the customer’s bot-stage choice, routinely overtaken by a live resolution.
Practice recommendation. When a customer leaves or stops responding after the agent connects, the agent should open a case rather than silently close the chat. That single behaviour change removes the bulk of the inflated “resolved-in-chat” count and ensures unresolved questions have a case to follow up.
